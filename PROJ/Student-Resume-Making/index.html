Resume Tips & Tricks

Expert advice to make your resume stand out

✅ Do's - What to Include

📌 Keep It Concise

Your resume should be one page for students. Save the detailed stories for interviews. Focus on relevant information only.

🎯 Use Strong Action Verbs

Start bullet points with verbs like "Developed," "Created," "Led," "Implementing" instead of passive language. This makes your achievements stand out.

📊 Quantify Your Achievements

Numbers speak louder than words. Instead of "improved performance," say "improved performance by 40%" or "managed 5+ projects."

🎓 Highlight Relevant Skills

Tailor your skills section to the job you're applying for. Include technical skills, programming languages, and soft skills relevant to the position.

💼 Include Real Experience

Add internships, freelance work, class projects, and volunteer work. Even academic projects demonstrate practical skills.

❌ Don'ts - What to Avoid

🚫 Spelling & Grammar Mistakes

Proofread multiple times! Spelling and grammar errors make you look careless. Use tools like Grammarly or ask a friend to review.

🚫 Personal Information Overload

Don't include age, photo, marital status, or personal interests unless specific to the job. Focus on professional qualifications.

🚫 Vague Descriptions

Avoid generic phrases like "responsible for" or "worked on." Be specific about what you did and its impact.

🚫 Too Much Information

Don't include high school experience if you're in college, or list every small project. Keep it relevant and recent.

🚫 Unprofessional Email Address

Use a professional email like firstname.lastname@email.com, not something like "partydude@email.com" or "coder123@email.com."

🚫 Inconsistent Formatting

Keep fonts, spacing, and bullet styles consistent throughout. A messy resume is hard to read and looks unprofessional.
